Information Security Analyst


What do they do? Click here to find out

An Information security analyst primary responsibility is to secure and safe guard information. They Set up the security of information systems to protect form cyber attacks and network infiltration. It can be set up through the computer system itself or on the servers hosting the Local Area Network (LAN) or Wide Area Network (WAN).

Information security analyst can prevent, monitor, and respond to any type of malware or cyber attack. With the increase of technology over the last decade there has been an equal rise in cyber attacks. These attacks can cost companies thousands if not millions of dollars. Not to mention the breach of customers and/or employees’ personal information.

Due to this more companies need to secure their data. This is why the projected job growth for Information security analyst is 31.6 percent over the next ten years (2018 – 2028). This is higher than most. For perspective, the job growth over the same time period for computer related occupations in 12 percent and 5 percent for all occupations.


Knowledge and Skills Required

Most Information security analyst have a bachelor’s degree in computer related studies. Mostly this degree is in computer science or programing. Some places may even want you to have a master in business administration.

Generally, most Information security analyst have experience in a related occupation. This is usually a network or computer systems administrator. Employers want to make sure they are hiring a person that can work with the systems they have.

It is always helpful to have technical certifications on network admiration or software development. These certifications could cost a few hundred but will look good to future employers and better on your resume. Some of the professional certifications your employer may pay for if they won’t you to have them. Most of the other training you will receive is on the job training.
